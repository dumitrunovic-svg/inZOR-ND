Abstract

We document the ZOR (2D/3D artificial-life) scene built around ethylene torsional / quasi-degenerate SA-CASSCF(2,2) physics: organisms evolve under a fitness signal derived from the S₀–S₁ gap (and related observables), with optional local geometry sweeps and ND ambient extensions. This page is not an inZOR-ND active-space search report; it complements the H₂ gap diagnostic and the inZOR-ND N₂/Cr₂ benchmarks by showing how the same chemical motif (gap pathology near degeneracy) appears in an evolutionary explorer.

Engine boundary. Code, logs, and batch runners live in the ZOR repository (zor_scenes/scene_ethylene_quasidegen/, zor_active_space/). inZOR-ND results for automatic CAS selection remain under the separate active-space test pages linked from the chapter hub.

Protocol (summary)

  • Electronic structure: SA-CASSCF(2,2) with cc-pVDZ (consistent with other gap experiments in this chapter unless noted in logs).
  • Geometry: internal-coordinate drives (e.g. torsion / soft modes) with the quantum probe evaluated on population proposals.
  • Validation: multi-seed batches, control baselines, local grid checks; extensions to 3D ambient fields where ZOR_ETHYLENE_ND / ND folders are enabled.
  • Score variants: legacy, local_coherent, dwell_coherent — compared in validation logs to test robustness of the fitness landscape, not to replace QC baselines.
