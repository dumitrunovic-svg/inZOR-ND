Abstract

We compare the state-averaged CASSCF(2,2) HOMO–LUMO gap along an H–H distance scan when active orbitals are picked by inZOR-ND (fixed MO indices per method) versus a NOON-style heuristic pool. At stretched geometries the NOON-selected gap trace shows a large excursion (loss of monotonicity), while the inZOR-ND trace remains monotone decreasing in this tabulated run. This does not replace full PEC benchmarks; it isolates gap behaviour as a cheap diagnostic aligned with the wider active-space chapter.
